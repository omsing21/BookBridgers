BookBridgers is a digital platform designed to connect students, libraries, and donors in a seamless ecosystem, with the goal of making textbooks and educational materials accessible to all. Through intuitive search, reservation, and donation modules, the system empowers students to request books, allows donors to list and track contributions, and helps libraries optimize their inventory and outreach. This coordinated network seeks to eliminate access barriers and address inequalities in educational resource distribution.

Aligned with UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) 4, 10, 12, and 17, the project contributes by promoting Quality Education (SDG 4) through broader access to learning tools; by Reducing Inequalities (SDG 10) as it levels the playing field for students from varied socioeconomic backgrounds; by encouraging Responsible Consumption & Production (SDG 12) via reuse of books and reducing waste; and by fostering Partnerships (SDG 17) among individuals, institutions, and communities working together for educational equity.

Built using a modern three-layer architecture (frontend UI, backend API, relational database), BookBridgers supports modular development—user registration, book search, request flow, donation tracking, and geolocation for pickup/drop logistics. Future expansions include AI-based recommendations, mobile apps, and analytics dashboards, enabling the platform to grow in impact and sustainability over time.
